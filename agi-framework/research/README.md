# AGI Framework — Research

## Purpose

Research the theoretical foundations and state of the art for AGI architecture, with a focus on approaches that can be grounded in human life prediction rather than abstract benchmarks.

## Active Research Areas

### World Models
- [ ] Survey: World model architectures (Dreamer, I-JEPA, Gato, etc.)
- [ ] Continual learning and catastrophic forgetting prevention
- [ ] Multi-modal world models (structured data + natural language)

### Causal Reasoning
- [ ] Pearl causal hierarchy and its application to prediction systems
- [ ] Causal discovery from observational data
- [ ] Counterfactual reasoning for "what if I change X" queries
- [ ] Integration of causal models with machine learning

### Generalization
- [ ] Few-shot and zero-shot prediction in structured domains
- [ ] Transfer learning across prediction domains
- [ ] Analogical reasoning for novel prediction types

### Uncertainty
- [ ] Calibrated uncertainty in complex prediction systems
- [ ] Second-order uncertainty ("I don't know how uncertain I am")
- [ ] Communication of uncertainty to non-expert users

### Evaluation
- [ ] How do we evaluate AGI-level reasoning in the context of life prediction?
- [ ] Benchmarks for cross-domain reasoning quality
- [ ] Human evaluation protocols for holistic life modeling

## Key Research Questions

1. What is the minimum architecture needed for genuine cross-domain causal reasoning?
2. Can we build a world model that updates continuously from heterogeneous prediction domains?
3. How do we evaluate generalization to novel prediction types?
4. What does "alignment" mean in the context of a system designed to optimize human life outcomes?

# Vision

## The Problem

Accurate predictions about human life — health risks, learning trajectories, financial outcomes, environmental exposures — are available today. But they exist behind expensive clinical tools, institutional databases, and proprietary systems that most people can never access. The gap between what is knowable and what is known by ordinary people represents one of the largest preventable sources of human suffering.

## The Vision

**Every person on Earth should have access to high-quality, personalized predictions about every important aspect of their life — for free, forever.**

Not predictions from a single company's black box. Predictions built by the global community, verified by the global community, owned by nobody — and by everybody.

## Roadmap

### Phase 1: Scaffold (Current)
Establish the architecture. Define domains, project interfaces, data collection standards, accuracy tracking standards, and contribution norms. Create the framework that thousands of contributors can build within.

### Phase 2: Foundation Projects
Implement the first working prediction project in each domain. Establish data pipelines, baseline models, accuracy tracking loops, and self-correction mechanisms. Prove the architecture works end-to-end.

### Phase 3: Collective Intelligence
Aggregate project outputs into domain-level predictions. Build the cross-domain synthesis layer that creates holistic life models — understanding how health predictions inform financial ones, how early-life predictions shape aging outcomes.

### Phase 4: AGI Foundation
Use the collective intelligence layer as the scaffold for reasoning about the full human condition. AGI emerges not from abstract benchmarks but from modeling real human life across all its dimensions.

### Phase 5: Hardware + Robotics
When capable robots are available, extend predictions into physical interventions — not just predicting health decline, but acting on it. Not just predicting accident risk, but preventing accidents.

## Core Principles

**Open by default.** All core work is open source. No exceptions, no expiration.

**Free forever.** The baseline prediction service is always free. No paywalls for life-critical information.

**Global participation.** No geography, credential, or affiliation requirement. A researcher in Lagos and an engineer in Seoul contribute on equal footing.

**Self-improving.** Every prediction system tracks its own accuracy and improves continuously. No system is considered done; all systems are considered in training.

**Human-centered AGI.** We believe AGI should emerge from understanding human life, not from optimizing abstract benchmarks. This project is our path toward that.

**Near-zero cost.** Technology — open data, open models, distributed compute, community labor — should make global-scale prediction essentially free to operate.

## On Proprietary Products

Building a paid product on this foundation that genuinely helps people is not a contradiction of the mission — it is evidence that the mission works. If you improve people's lives and earn money doing it, that sustains the broader development. The constraints are minimal: you cannot close the baseline, and you cannot exclude people from the free layer. Everything else is fair game.

## On AGI

We do not believe AGI will emerge from scaling a single objective function on text. We believe it will emerge from systems that can reason simultaneously about health, economics, relationships, environment, behavior, and the interactions between them — the full complexity of what it means to be human. This project builds that substrate from the bottom up, starting from real human needs.

## The Scale of the Opportunity

8 billion people. One lifespan each. Hundreds of prediction types that could matter to each person at each stage of life. The surface area of this problem is larger than any single organization can address. That is why it must be built by the world, for the world.
